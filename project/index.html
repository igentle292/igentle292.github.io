Speed of Speedrunning

By Isaiah Gentle


An exploration of the players and progression of Speedrunning in Super Mario 64

Background

I have been a fan of speedrunning videos and streams for several years. I have watched speedrun content for many different games. I have watched major speedrunning events like Games Done Quick. And I have watched many videos that analyze the history of speedrunning, many from the creator SummoningSalt(highly recommend!). But today I want to explore more about speedrunning and it's history myself, specifically with the most popular "speed game," Super Mario 64.


But what is Speedrunning?

Speedrunning is the act of completing a video game as quickly as possible. This often includes the use of glitches and exploits to skip major sections or challenges of the game. Most games have multiple categories, with different goals or methods. The most common are "any%," which requires beating the game as quickly as possible, with no restrictions, and "100%," which requires completing every major task in the game as quickly as possible. In the context of Super Mario 64, normal completion of the game requires getting 70 "Power Stars" before you complete the final level. "any%" is done by collecting 0 Power Stars, and is done in about 6 minutes. "100%" is done by collecting all 120 Power Stars, and is done in about an hour and a half. Today, I will explore the "100%" category.

How active is the speedrunning community?

Even if a speedrunning community is popular, the commnuity may not be active. Thankfully, speedrun.com keeps a count of how many speedrunners have contributed to the community within the last month, so we can see how popular it is.

This community is still up and running, even in a game that is nearing 30 years of age.
